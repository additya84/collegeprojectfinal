Resume Writing Tips

Your resume is the first impression you create on recruiters. Before interviews happen, your resume decides whether you will be shortlisted or not.

Recruiters generally spend only a few seconds scanning a resume, therefore clarity, structure and relevance are extremely important.

A good resume highlights your skills, projects, education, achievements and strengths in a professional manner.

Ideal Resume Structure

1. Personal Information

Include:

  • Full Name
  • Email Address
  • Phone Number
  • Location
  • LinkedIn Profile
  • GitHub Profile

2. Career Objective

Write a short and professional objective explaining your career goals and interests.

3. Education

Mention your latest qualification first along with CGPA, percentage and institution details.

4. Skills

Only include skills that you can explain confidently during interviews.

Top Resume Tips

✔ Keep resume limited to one page for freshers.

✔ Use professional formatting and fonts.

✔ Highlight projects and technical skills.

✔ Use action words such as Developed, Designed and Built.

✔ Keep content concise and relevant.

✔ Proofread before sending the resume.

✔ Use professional email addresses.

ATS Resume Tips

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter resumes automatically.

To improve ATS compatibility:

  • Use standard headings.
  • Avoid tables and images.
  • Use keywords from job descriptions.
  • Keep formatting simple.

Common Resume Mistakes

❌ Adding fake skills.

❌ Using unprofessional email addresses.

❌ Including spelling mistakes.

❌ Writing long paragraphs.

❌ Mentioning technologies that cannot be explained.

❌ Including unnecessary personal information.

Final Resume Checklist

✔ Contact information updated.

✔ Grammar checked.

✔ Projects explained properly.

✔ Skills verified.

✔ Resume saved as PDF.

✔ File name is professional.
