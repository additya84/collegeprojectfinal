🚶 Data Structure

Queue

Learn Queue Data Structure including Queue operations, Circular Queue and applications with examples.

📘 What is a Queue?

Queue is a linear data structure that follows the FIFO (First In First Out) principle.

The element inserted first is removed first.

📚 Queue Representation

Front → 10 | 20 | 30 | 40 ← Rear

Insertion takes place at Rear and deletion takes place at Front.

➕ Enqueue Operation

Enqueue means inserting an element into the queue. 

Before:
10 | 20 | 30

Enqueue 40

After:
10 | 20 | 30 | 40

➖ Dequeue Operation

Dequeue means removing an element from the queue. 

Before:
10 | 20 | 30 | 40

Dequeue

After:
20 | 30 | 40

🔄 Circular Queue

Circular Queue connects the last position back to the first position to utilize memory efficiently. 

10 → 20 → 30 → 40
↑              ↓
└──────────────┘

💻 Queue Implementation in C

#define SIZE 5

int queue[SIZE];
int front = -1;
int rear = -1;

🌍 Applications of Queue

  • CPU Scheduling
  • Printer Queue
  • Call Center Systems

✅ Advantages of Queue

  • Efficient data processing.
  • Maintains order of elements.
  • Useful in scheduling systems.

❌ Disadvantages of Queue

  • No random access to elements.
  • Searching is slower.
  • Fixed size in array implementation.
