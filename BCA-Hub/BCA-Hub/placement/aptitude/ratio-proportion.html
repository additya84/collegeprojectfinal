Ratio and Proportion

Ratio and Proportion is one of the most important topics for Placement Aptitude, SSC, Banking, Railway and CAT exams. Questions are generally based on age, partnership, mixture, salary distribution, coins and proportional relationships. A strong understanding of this chapter helps in solving many arithmetic questions quickly.

  • Important Formulas

Ratio & Proportion Formulas

Basic Formulas

Basic Ratio: A : B = A/B

Duplicate Ratio: A² : B²

Sub-Duplicate Ratio: √A : √B

Triplicate Ratio: A³ : B³

Sub-Triplicate Ratio: ∛A : ∛B

Compound Ratio: AC : BD

Fourth Proportional: X = (B × C)/A

Third Proportional: X = B²/A

Mean Proportional: X = √(A × B)

Continued Proportion: B² = AC

Shortcut Tricks

N Method:
A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5
A:B:C = 8:12:15

Neighbour Method:
A:B:C:D = 3:6:8:12

Hide Method:
2A = 3B = 4C
A:B:C = 6:4:3

Cross Multiplication:
(3×23) - (5×12) = 9 units

Coin Trick:
₹1 ×1, 50p ×2, 25p ×4

Age Trick:
Difference in ratio parts = Actual age difference.

Option Elimination:
(A+X)/(B+X) = (C+X)/(D+X)

Ratio & Proportion Questions

Q1. The ratio of present ages of Maya and Naman is 4:3. Total ages of Maya, Naman and Kabir are 76 years. Find Naman's age.

Maya = 4x, Naman = 3x.
After solving, x = 6.
Naman = 3 × 6 = 18 years.
Answer = 18 years.

Q2. ₹9000 is divided among X, Y and Z in the ratio 4:5:6. Find the difference between X and Z.

Total parts = 15.
1 part = 9000/15 = 600.
Difference = (6−4) × 600 = 1200.
Answer = ₹1200.

Q3. Divide ₹782 in the ratio 1/2 : 2/3 : 3/4. Find first part.

LCM = 12.
Ratio = 6 : 8 : 9.
Total parts = 23.
1 part = 34.
First part = 6 × 34 = ₹204.
Answer = ₹204.

Q4. The ratio of two ages is 3:2 and the difference is 5 years. Find younger age.

Difference in ratio = 1 unit.
1 unit = 5 years.
Younger age = 2 × 5 = 10 years.
Answer = 10 years.

Q5. ₹117 was wrongly divided in the ratio 2:3:4 instead of 1/2 : 1/3 : 1/4. Who gains the most?

Correct ratio = 6:4:3.
Wrong ratio = 2:3:4.
R gains ₹25 which is maximum.
Answer = R, ₹25.

Q6. u:v = 4:7 and v:w = 9:7. If u = 72, find w.

u:w = 36:49.
w = (49×72)/36 = 98.
Answer = 98.

Q7. Ravi and Sarita salaries are in ratio 3:5. After increasing both by ₹5000, ratio becomes 29:45. Find Sarita's salary.

(3x+5000)/(5x+5000)=29/45.
x = 8000.
Sarita = 5x = ₹40000.
Answer = ₹40,000.

Q8. A bag contains ₹785 in ₹2, ₹5 and ₹10 coins in ratio 6:9:10. Find number of ₹5 coins.

2(6x)+5(9x)+10(10x)=785.
157x=785.
x=5.
₹5 coins = 9x = 45.
Answer = 45 coins.

Q9. A man has 25p, 50p and ₹1 coins. Total coins are 220 and total amount is ₹160. Find number of 50p coins.

Let 25p coins = x.
₹1 coins = 3x.
50p coins = 220−4x.
x=40.
50p coins = 60.
Answer = 60 coins.

Q10. If A:B = 7:8 and B:C = 7:9, find A:B:C.

A:B = 49:56.
B:C = 56:72.
Therefore,
A:B:C = 49:56:72.
