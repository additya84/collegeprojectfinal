Permutation & Combination

Learn concepts, formulas, shortcuts and exam level questions for placement preparation and aptitude tests.

Important Formulas

1. Factorial

n! = n × (n-1) × (n-2) × ... × 1

0! = 1

2. Permutation Formula

nPr = n! / (n-r)!

3. Combination Formula

nCr = n! / r!(n-r)!

4. Relation Between P and C

nPr = nCr × r!

5. Property

nCr = nC(n-r)

6. Special Values

nC0 = 1

nCn = 1

nC1 = n

7. Pascal Rule

nCr + nC(r-1) = (n+1)Cr

8. Permutation With Repetition

Ways = n! / (p! × q!)

9. Circular Permutation

People Around Table = (n-1)!

10. Garland / Necklace Case

Ways = (n-1)! / 2

11. Selection From Distinct Items

Total Ways = 2ⁿ - 1

12. Selection From Identical Items

(p+1)(q+1)(r+1) - 1

13. Number Of Diagonals

n(n-3) / 2

14. Number Of Triangles

nC3

15. Straight Lines From n Points

nC2

Shortcut Tricks

1. Box Method

Use boxes equal to the number of places and multiply options.

Example:
3 digit numbers from 3,5,7,8
4 × 3 × 2 = 24

2. String / Tie Method

If some persons or letters must stay together, treat them as one unit.

Example:
[UE], J, D, G
4! × 2! = 48

3. Gap Method

Arrange remaining persons first and place restricted persons in gaps.

Example:
5 Boys and 4 Girls
No two girls together
Answer = 5! × 6P4

4. Countdown Trick

For nCr calculation use countdown method.

10C3 =
(10 × 9 × 8) / (3 × 2 × 1)
Answer = 120

5. Handshake Trick

Total Handshakes = nC2

Formula:
n(n-1)/2

6. Gift Exchange Trick

Total Gifts = nP2

Formula:
n(n-1)

Solved Questions

Q1. A committee of 5 members is to be formed from 7 men and 5 women. Find the number of committees containing at least 3 women.

Case 1:
3 Women and 2 Men
5C3 × 7C2
10 × 21 = 210

Case 2:
4 Women and 1 Man
5C4 × 7C1
5 × 7 = 35

Case 3:
5 Women and 0 Men
5C5 × 7C0
1 × 1 = 1

Total Ways
210 + 35 + 1
= 246

Q2. In how many ways can the letters of the word QUEUE be arranged such that Q is immediately followed by U?

Treat QU as one unit.

Units become:
[QU], E, U, E

Total letters = 4
Two E's are identical.

Required Ways
= 4! / 2!
= 24 / 2
= 12

Q3. In how many ways can 5 different balls be distributed into 3 different boxes such that no box remains empty?

Total distributions
= 3⁵
= 243

Subtract cases where one box is empty.

Exactly one box empty:
3 × 2⁵
= 96

Add cases where two boxes are empty:
3 × 1⁵
= 3

Required Ways
= 243 - 96 + 3
= 150

Q4. A person wants to invite one or more friends out of his 8 friends. In how many ways can he do so?

Total number of friends = 8

For selection problems:
Total Ways = 2ⁿ - 1

Total Ways = 2⁸ - 1
= 256 - 1
= 255

Final Answer: 255 ways

Q5. Six friends are sitting in a row. Two of them are best friends and do not want to sit together. Find the number of arrangements.

Total arrangements of 6 friends
= 6!
= 720

Treat the two friends as one unit.
Units become = 5

Arrangements when together
= 5! × 2!
= 120 × 2
= 240

Required arrangements
= 720 - 240
= 480

Final Answer: 480 ways

Q6. How many two digit numbers can be formed using 3, 5 and 7 if repetition is allowed?

First digit options = 3
Second digit options = 3

Using Box Method:

3 × 3
= 9

Final Answer: 9 numbers

Q7. In a meeting every person shakes hands with every other person. If total handshakes are 210, find the number of persons present.

Handshake Formula:
n(n-1)/2 = 210

n(n-1) = 420

20 × 21 = 420

Therefore,
n = 21

Final Answer: 21 persons

Q8. In how many ways can the letters of the word GEOGRAPHY be arranged such that all vowels always remain together?

Letters in GEOGRAPHY = 8
Vowels = E, O, A
Consonants = G, G, R, P, H, Y

Treat all vowels as one unit.

Total units become:
[Vowels], G, G, R, P, H, Y
Total Units = 7

Since G repeats twice:
Ways to arrange units
= 7! / 2!
= 2520

Arrange vowels internally:
3!
= 6

Total Arrangements
= 2520 × 6
= 15120

Final Answer: 15120

Q9. A committee of 5 members is to be selected from 7 men and 5 women such that the committee contains at least 3 men.

3 Men and 2 Women

35 × 10

Case 2:
4 Men and 1 Woman
7C4 × 5C1
35 × 5
= 175

Case 3:
5 Men and 0 Women
7C5 × 5C0
21 × 1
= 21

Total Ways
= 350 + 175 + 21
= 546

Final Answer: 546

Q10. What is the probability that all vowels occupy even positions in the word ARTICLE?

Letters in ARTICLE = 7
Vowels = A, I, E
Even positions available = 2, 4, 6

Ways to arrange vowels in even positions
= 3!
= 6

Remaining consonants:
R, T, C, L

Ways to arrange consonants
= 4!
= 24

Favourable arrangements
= 6 × 24
= 144

Total arrangements
= 7!
= 5040

Probability
= 144 / 5040
= 1 / 35

Final Answer: 1/35
