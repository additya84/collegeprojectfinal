Time and Work

Time and Work is one of the most important topics for Placement Aptitude, SSC, Banking, Railway and CAT exams. Questions are generally based on efficiency, men and women, pipes and cisterns, wages distribution and alternate day work.

Time and Work Formulas

Important Formulas

Work Done: Work = Time × Efficiency

Efficiency: Efficiency = 1 / Time Taken

Two Persons Working Together:

Time = (x × y)/(x + y)

Three Persons Working Together:

Time = xyz/(xy + yz + zx)

Finding One Person's Time:

B's Time = (x × y)/(y - x)

MDH Formula:

(M₁×D₁×H₁×E₁)/W₁=(M₂×D₂×H₂×E₂)/W₂

Pipes Formula:

Time=(x×y)/(y-x)

Shortcut Tricks

1. Use LCM Method instead of fractions.

2. Efficiency and Time are inversely proportional.

3. 50% more efficient means efficiency ratio 3:2.

4. Solve alternate day questions using cycle method.

5. Wages are distributed according to work done ratio.

6. Leakage efficiency is considered negative.

7. Use Men-Women shortcut formula in exam questions.

Detailed Solved Questions

Q1. A can complete a work in 10 days and B can complete the same work in 15 days. In how many days can they complete the work together?

LCM of 10 and 15 = 30 Units

A's efficiency = 30/10 = 3 units per day
B's efficiency = 30/15 = 2 units per day

Together efficiency = 3 + 2 = 5 units per day

Time Taken = Total Work / Total Efficiency
Time = 30/5 = 6 Days

Final Answer = 6 Days

Q2. A can complete a work in 12 days while B can complete the same work in 18 days. Find the time taken if both work together.

LCM of 12 and 18 = 36 Units

A's efficiency = 36/12 = 3 units/day
B's efficiency = 36/18 = 2 units/day

Combined efficiency = 5 units/day

Time Taken = 36/5
Time Taken = 7.2 Days

Final Answer = 7.2 Days

Q3. A is 50% more efficient than B. If B completes the work in 18 days, in how many days can A complete the work?

If A is 50% more efficient than B,
Efficiency Ratio = 3 : 2
Time Ratio = 2 : 3

B takes 18 days
A takes = (18 × 2)/3
A takes = 12 days

Final Answer = 12 Days

Q4. A pipe fills a tank in 8 hours while another pipe empties it in 12 hours. Find the time required to fill the tank if both pipes are opened together.

Net Time Formula:
(8 × 12)/(12 - 8)

= 96/4
= 24 Hours

Final Answer = 24 Hours

Q5. A and B together complete a work in 20 days. If they work for 15 days, what fraction of work is completed?

Total Work = 20 Units
Work done in 1 day = 1 Unit
Work done in 15 days = 15 Units

Fraction Completed = 15/20
Fraction Completed = 3/4

Final Answer = 3/4 Work Completed

Q6. A pipe fills a tank in 15 hours. What fraction of the tank will be filled in 3 hours?

Work done in 1 hour = 1/15
Work done in 3 hours = 3/15
= 1/5

Final Answer = 1/5 of Tank Filled

Q7. A can complete a work in 20 days and B in 30 days. Find their combined time.

LCM = 60 Units
A efficiency = 3 units/day
B efficiency = 2 units/day
Combined efficiency = 5 units/day
Time = 60/5 = 12 Days

Final Answer = 12 Days

Q8. A alone completes work in 24 days while A and B together complete it in 16 days. Find B's time alone.

B's Time Formula:
(24 × 16)/(24 - 16)
= 384/8
= 48 Days

Final Answer = 48 Days

Q9. A worker completes 3/5 of work and leaves. Another worker completes remaining work. If total wages are ₹5000, find first worker's share.

First worker completed = 3/5 work
Wages are distributed according to work done.

First worker's share:
(3/5) × 5000
= ₹3000

Final Answer = ₹3000

Q10. 12 men can complete a work in 15 days. In how many days will 20 men complete the same work?

Men × Days = Constant
12 × 15 = 20 × D
180 = 20D
D = 9 Days

Final Answer = 9 Days
