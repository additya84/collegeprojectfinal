Profit & Loss

Learn complete Profit & Loss concepts, formulas, shortcut tricks and placement level questions.

What is Profit & Loss?

Profit and Loss is one of the most important topics in Quantitative Aptitude and is frequently asked in placement exams, banking exams, SSC, railway exams and competitive tests.

Questions are generally based on Cost Price, Selling Price, Marked Price, Discount, Successive Discount and Dishonest Dealer concepts.

If the Selling Price is greater than Cost Price, the seller earns a Profit.

If the Selling Price is less than Cost Price, the seller suffers a Loss.

Basic Terms

Cost Price (CP)

The price at which an article is purchased.

Selling Price (SP)

The price at which an article is sold.

Marked Price (MP)

The price printed on the label of the article.

Discount

The reduction given on the Marked Price.

Formula Sheet

Profit Formula

Profit = SP - CP

Loss Formula

Loss = CP - SP

Profit Percentage

Profit % = (Profit ├Ę CP) ├Ś 100

Loss Percentage

Loss % = (Loss ├Ę CP) ├Ś 100

Selling Price at Profit

SP = CP ├Ś ((100 + Profit %) ├Ę 100)

Selling Price at Loss

SP = CP ├Ś ((100 - Loss %) ├Ę 100)

Cost Price at Profit

CP = (SP ├Ś 100) ├Ę (100 + Profit %)

Cost Price at Loss

CP = (SP ├Ś 100) ├Ę (100 - Loss %)

Discount Formula

Discount = MP - SP

Discount Percentage

Discount % = (Discount ├Ę MP) ├Ś 100

Selling Price using Discount

SP = MP ├Ś ((100 - Discount %) ├Ę 100)

MP to CP Ratio Formula

MP ├Ę CP = (100 + Profit %) ├Ę (100 - Discount %)

CP to MP Ratio Formula

Solved Examples

Q1. A businessman gives a discount of 15% on the marked price and still earns a profit of 19%. Find the percentage above cost price at which he marked the item.

Let CP = Ōé╣100
Profit = 19%

Final Answer: 40%

Q2. A shopkeeper buys an article for Ōé╣800 and sells it for Ōé╣1000. Find the profit and profit percentage.

Q6. A dealer claims to sell goods at Cost Price but gives only 900g instead of 1kg. Find his profit percentage.
