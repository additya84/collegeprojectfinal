Probability Formulas

Basic Probability

P(A)= Favourable Outcomes / Total Outcomes

P(A)=n(E)/n(S)

Complementary Event

P(A') = 1 - P(A)

Addition Rule

P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A∩B)

Mutually Exclusive Events

P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)

Multiplication Rule

P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B)

Conditional Probability

P(A|B)=P(A∩B)/P(B)

Odds in Favour

Odds = Favourable / Unfavourable

Odds Against

Odds = Unfavourable / Favourable

Binomial Distribution

P(X=r)=ⁿCr × pʳ × qⁿ⁻ʳ

Total Probability Rule

P(A)=P(B1)P(A|B1)+P(B2)P(A|B2)+...

Probability Tricks

Trick 1 : At Least One Trick

P(At least one) = 1 - P(None)
Example:
3 coins toss and at least one head.
Answer = 1 - (1/8) = 7/8

Trick 2 : Dice Sum Formula

Sum 2 to 7 → Ways = Sum - 1
Sum 8 to 12 → Ways = 13 - Sum

Trick 3 : Three Dice Shortcut

Sum 10 and Sum 11 both have 27 ways.
Probability = 27/216 = 1/8

Trick 4 : Calendar Trick

Ordinary Year → 1/7
Leap Year → 2/7

Trick 5 : Exact Heads Formula

Probability = nCr / 2ⁿ

Trick 6 : Cards Without Replacement

Multiply probabilities directly.
Example:
4/52 × 3/51 = 1/221

Trick 7 : Colored Balls Method

Probability = Desired Selection / Total Selection
Example:
5C2 / 9C2 = 5/18

Probability Solved Questions

Q1. A random sample of 2 items is selected from 15 items. Find total samples.

Formula : nCr
15C2 = (15×14)/(2×1)
= 105
Answer = 105

Q2. Five cards numbered 1,3,5,6,9 are used to form 5 digit numbers. Find probability that number is divisible by 4.

Total numbers = 5! =120
Favourable cases =24
Probability =24/120
Answer =0.2

Q3. Mean and variance of binomial distribution are 5 and 4 respectively. Find probability of success.

Mean=np=5
Variance=npq=4
5q=4
q=4/5
p=1/5
Answer =0.2

Q4. A box contains 4 red and 6 blue balls. Find probability of selecting 2 balls of same colour.

Probability=(4C2+6C2)/10C2
=(6+15)/45
=21/45
=7/15
Answer =7/15

Q5. A bag contains 7 red and 4 blue balls. Find probability of different colours.

P=(7/11×4/11)+(4/11×7/11)
=56/121
Answer =56/121

Q6. P(B)=0.4 and P(A∪B)=0.6 If A and B are independent find P(A).

P(A)+P(B)-P(A)P(B)=0.6
P(A)+0.4-0.4P(A)=0.6
0.6P(A)=0.2
P(A)=1/3

Q7. P(A)=0.4 and P(A∪B)=0.7 Find P(B) if A and B are independent.

0.7=0.4+P-0.4P
0.6P=0.3
P=0.5

Q8. Eight couples are in a room. Four persons are selected randomly. Find probability they are couples.

Required Probability= 8C2 /16C4

Q9. Three mangoes and three apples are in a box. Find probability one mango and one apple are selected.

Favourable=3C1×3C1=9
Total=6C2=15
Probability=9/15
Answer=3/5

Q10. An unbiased coin is tossed three times. Given third toss is Head. Find probability of at least one more Head.

Possible cases:
HHH,HHT,HTH,TTH
Favourable=3
Total=4
Probability=3/4
