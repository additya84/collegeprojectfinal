Simple Interest

Simple Interest is one of the most important chapters for Placement Preparation, Banking, SSC, Railway and Competitive Exams. Questions are generally based on Principal, Rate, Time, Amount, Investments and Interest Comparison.

In this chapter you will learn:

  • Important Formulas
  • Shortcut Tricks
  • Detailed Solved Questions
  • Recommended Videos
  • Downloadable Notes and PDFs

Formula Sheet

Simple Interest Formula

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Total Amount Formula

A = P + SI

A = P(1 + RT/100)

Principal Formula

P = (A × 100) / (100 + RT)

Rate Formula

R = (SI × 100) / (P × T)

Time Formula

T = (SI × 100) / (P × R)

Days to Years Conversion

T = Days / 365

Months to Years Conversion

T = Months / 12

Installment Formula

Annual Installment = (100 × A) / [100t + Rt(t-1)/2]

Shortcut Tricks

n-1 Trick

R = ((n - 1) × 100) / T

Times Comparison Trick

T1/T2 = (n1-1)/(n2-1)

Rate × Time Trick

Total SI Percentage = Rate × Time

Change in Interest

ΔSI = (P × ΔR × T)/100

1 Year Interest Trick

1 Year SI = (A2 - A1)/(T2 - T1)

Alligation Method

Used for splitting principal into different investments.

Equal Interest Ratio

P1:P2 = 1/(R1T1) : 1/(R2T2)

Solved Questions

Q1. A sum doubles itself in 10 years at Simple Interest. In how many years will it become three times itself?

When money doubles:
Interest = Principal

Therefore,
100% interest is earned in 10 years.

To become three times,
Interest required = 200%

Time required:
10 × 2 = 20 years

Final Answer = 20 Years

Q2. A sum becomes ₹585 in 5 years at 6% Simple Interest. Find the Principal.

Amount = ₹585
Rate = 6%
Time = 5 years

P = (A × 100)/(100 + RT)

P = (585 × 100)/(100 + 30)
P = 58500/130
P = ₹450

Final Answer = ₹450

Q3. Find the Simple Interest on ₹3600 for 10 years at 5%.

SI = (P × R × T)/100

SI = (3600 × 5 × 10)/100
SI = ₹1800

Final Answer = ₹1800

Q4. Find the Amount on ₹8000 for 4 years at 8% SI.

SI = (8000 × 8 × 4)/100
SI = ₹2560

Amount = 8000 + 2560
Amount = ₹10560

Final Answer = ₹10560

Q5. At what rate will ₹4000 earn ₹1200 in 5 years?

R = (SI × 100)/(P × T)

R = (1200 × 100)/(4000 × 5)
R = 6%

Final Answer = 6%

Q6. In how many years will ₹5000 earn ₹1500 at 10% SI?

T = (SI × 100)/(P × R)

T = (1500 × 100)/(5000 × 10)
T = 3 years

Final Answer = 3 Years

Q7. A sum triples itself in 12 years. Find the rate.

Use n-1 Trick:

R = ((3-1) × 100)/12
R = 16.67%

Final Answer = 16.67%

Q8. ₹6000 is invested for 3 years at 7% SI. Find interest.

SI = (6000 × 7 × 3)/100
SI = ₹1260

Final Answer = ₹1260

Q9. The difference between Amount and Principal is ₹2400. Find SI.

Amount = Principal + SI

SI = Amount - Principal
SI = ₹2400

Final Answer = ₹2400

Q10. A sum becomes ₹7500 from ₹5000 in 5 years. Find SI.

SI = Amount - Principal

SI = 7500 - 5000
SI = ₹2500

Final Answer = ₹2500
