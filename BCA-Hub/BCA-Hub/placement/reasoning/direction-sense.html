Direction Sense

Direction Sense is one of the most important reasoning topics for Placement, SSC, Banking and Railway exams. Questions are based on directions, movement, distance and turning.

  • Direction Rules
  • Shortcut Tricks
  • 10 Solved Questions
  • PDF Notes

Important Rules

Basic Directions

North (N)

South (S)

East (E)

West (W)

Turning Rules

Facing North + Right Turn = East

Facing North + Left Turn = West

Facing East + Right Turn = South

Facing East + Left Turn = North

Opposite Directions

North ↔ South

East ↔ West

Shortcut Trick

Always draw arrows while solving questions.

Shortcut Tricks

1. Draw directions immediately.

2. Use arrows instead of writing text.

3. Remember NESW clockwise order.

4. Right turn means clockwise movement.

5. Left turn means anti-clockwise movement.

Solved Questions

Q1.

Ravi walks 10m North and then 10m East. In which direction is he from the starting point?

Answer: North-East

Q2.

A person is facing North. He turns right and then right again. Which direction is he facing now?

Answer: South

Q3.

A man walks 15m South and then 20m East. Where is he now from starting point?

Answer: South-East

Q4.

A person walks North, turns left and walks further. Which direction is he moving now?

Answer: West

Q5.

A person is facing East. He turns left twice. What is his final direction?

Answer: West

Q6.

A person faces South and turns right. Which direction is he facing now?

Answer: West

Q7.

A person walks 10m East, then 10m North and then 10m West. How far is he from starting point?

Answer: 10 metres

Q8.

Facing West, a man turns left and then right. Which direction is he facing now?

Answer: West

Q9.

A boy walks 5m North, 5m East and 5m South. Where is he from the starting point?

Answer: East

Q10.

A man walks 10m North, 10m West and 10m South. Where is he now?

Answer: West of starting point
